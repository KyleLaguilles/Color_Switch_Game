## Design Context

### Users
CSUN students and classmates experiencing a class project demo. Casual, low-stakes context — fun and energy matter more than formal polish. The professor also evaluates it, so it should look intentional and cohesive, not thrown together.

### Brand Personality
Energetic, bold, retro-arcade. Three words: **charged, chunky, nostalgic**.

### Aesthetic Direction
Arcade cabinet energy — think early-90s coin-op games reimagined with modern CSS. Bold colors (not the current muted navy), chunky rounded UI elements, strong typographic hierarchy with a display font that has personality. The existing dark-navy-with-red-accent direction is in the right neighborhood but feels too corporate/dashboard-y. Push it toward neon signage over a dark background: deep black or very dark purple base, vivid color accents, tactile button states.

Theme: **Dark** — arcade cabinets are always dark, and the colorful balls read best against a dark background.

Anti-references: anything that looks like a SaaS admin panel, medical dashboard, or generic to-do app.

### Design Principles
1. **Every element should feel like it belongs on an arcade machine** — chunky borders, bold type, vivid colors, tactile interactions.
2. **Color carries meaning** — the 5 ball colors (red, blue, green, yellow, purple) are the hero visuals; the UI chrome should not compete with them.
3. **Energy through contrast** — high contrast between the dark background and vivid accents; no muted grays or washed-out mid-tones.
4. **Feedback is instant and physical** — button presses, correct/wrong answers, and ball drops should all feel punchy and immediate.
5. **WCAG AA minimum** — color contrast ratios ≥ 4.5:1 for text, keyboard navigable, proper focus states.
